Mathematical Foundations I 50 topics · 9 units

Arithmetic through pre-algebra: whole numbers, integers, fractions, decimals, ratios and percents, exponents, and first steps with variables and equations.

Mathematical Foundations II 44 topics · 10 units

Algebra in earnest: linear equations and systems, functions, polynomials and factoring, quadratics, radicals, rational expressions, exponentials and logarithms, and sequences.

Mathematical Foundations III 43 topics · 9 units

Precalculus and single-variable calculus: trigonometry, complex numbers, vectors, limits, derivatives, integrals, and infinite series.

Methods of Proof 19 topics · 5 units

The bridge to advanced mathematics: logic, sets, and the standard proof techniques — direct proof, contrapositive, contradiction, cases, and induction.

Linear Algebra 28 topics · 7 units

Vectors, matrices, and linear maps: systems of equations, vector spaces, determinants, eigenvalues, orthogonality, and the decompositions that power applied mathematics.

Multivariable Calculus 23 topics · 4 units

Calculus in higher dimensions: partial derivatives, gradients, multiple integrals, and the vector calculus of line and surface integrals through the big theorems of Green, Stokes, and Gauss.

Discrete Mathematics 23 topics · 5 units

The mathematics of the discrete: counting, recurrences, graph theory, and number theory, with proofs throughout.

Abstract Algebra 20 topics · 3 units

Algebraic structures from the ground up: groups, subgroups, quotients, and homomorphisms, then rings and fields.

Probability & Statistics 27 topics · 4 units

Probability from axioms through distributions and limit theorems, then statistical inference: estimation, confidence intervals, hypothesis testing, and regression.

Differential Equations 21 topics · 4 units

Ordinary differential equations: first- and second-order methods, Laplace transforms, linear systems, qualitative analysis, and a first look at numerical methods.

Mathematics for Machine Learning 15 topics · 4 units

The mathematics that machine learning actually uses: matrix calculus and decompositions, optimization, probability for models, and the derivations behind regression, classification, and backpropagation.

Mathematical Methods for the Physical Sciences I 14 topics · 5 units

The working mathematics of physics and engineering: complex methods, oscillations, Fourier analysis, vector calculus in field theories, and the matrix methods behind coupled systems.

Mathematical Methods for the Physical Sciences II 16 topics · 4 units

Advanced methods: partial differential equations and boundary value problems, special functions, Green's functions, calculus of variations, and complex analysis with contour integration.
